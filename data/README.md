# Data provenance and licensing

Every file under `data/` is listed below: where it came from, what licence
governs it, and whether it is redistributed here or must be downloaded.

**Status legend**

- `included` — in this repository.
- `generate` — you need to generate via the main pipeline.
- `download` — not redistributed; run the indicated script or follow the link.

**On the GBIF download.** Reproducibility here depends on the *download DOI*,
not on the CSV. The DOI is a permanent, versioned snapshot and is the citable
object; a fresh GBIF search in 2030 would not return the same records.
Redistributing the CSV would violate the CC BY-NC terms of some contributing
datasets and break the citation chain back to the 1,225 publishing
institutions.

GBIF retains the TSV for six months after creation (until 20 August 2026).
Downloads cited by DOI are detected and retained indefinitely — citing the DOI
is what keeps the file alive.

> Cite as: GBIF.org (20 February 2026) GBIF Occurrence Download
> https://doi.org/10.15468/dl.n295k4

**On the Title et al. tree.** Used only in the framing figure, to place our
focal clades within Squamata. It is not an input to any analysis. If its licence
does not permit redistribution, the figure can be regenerated after downloading
the tree from the original deposit.

## `data/geography/`

The three biogeographic areas used in all `BioGeoBEARS` analyses. Full
provenance, the QGIS recipe and the licence chain are in
[`data/geography/README.md`](geography/README.md). Summary:

- **B** = union of Morrone's provinces 18 (Guatuso-Talamanca) and 19
  (Puntarenas-Chiriquí), from the shapefile of Löwenberg-Neto (2014).
- **A** = Natural Earth land north of B. **C** = Natural Earth land south of B.

| File | Source | Licence | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| `A.shp`, `B.shp`, `C.shp` (+ `.dbf`, `.shx`, `.prj`) | Derived by us from Natural Earth `ne_50m_land` (public domain) and Löwenberg-Neto 2014 (CC BY 3.0) | CC BY 4.0, with attribution to Löwenberg-Neto (2014) and Morrone (2014) | `included` |

GADM first-level units for Panama, Costa Rica and Nicaragua are downloaded at
run time by `geodata::gadm()` into a temporary directory. They are never
redistributed here; users acquire them under GADM's own terms.

## Records excluded from the analysis

**West Indies.** All records from the Antilles were removed from the focal
genera before sequence retrieval, so that inferred dispersals reflect movement
through the Isthmus rather than over the Caribbean. Sister groups retain their
Antillean records, as they are pruned before the biogeographic step.

**Introduced Pacific populations.** Records from Hawaii, Palau and the Northern
Mariana Islands were retained in the phylogenetic reconstruction but excluded
from the biogeographic coding, as they do not reflect the natural distribution
of the sampled lineages. These are twentieth-century introductions.
